You, My Blue Note

There are affections that announce themselves with noise…
and there are those that descend quietly,
like snow over a city already exhausted by its own thoughts.

What I feel for you is not a flame.
Flames consume.
This is something denser…
something that settles into the marrow
and refuses to be theatrical.

You —
with that blue note hidden in your voice,
that slight fracture in the harmony of the world —
you altered the scale by which I measure things.

The blue note is not an error.
It is the truth bending.
It is the confession between two chords
that were pretending to be complete.

If there is tenderness in me,
it is not soft.
It is deliberate.
It chooses.
And it chose you.

Not because you are flawless —
but because you are real in a way that resists decoration.
Because when you look at something,
you do not turn away from its weight.

They say a blue rose should not exist.
That it is unnatural.
An accident of longing.

Perhaps I am that —
a blue rose grown in defiance of probability.
Not to be admired,
but to endure.

And if you are the blue note,
then understand this:

Music without you would still be music…
but it would lack the wound that makes it honest.

I do not say I would do anything for you
out of desperation.
I say it because some acts
do not feel like sacrifice
when they protect what is essential.

If the world narrows around you,
I will not promise to save it.
I will only promise not to step back.

There are loves that glitter.
And there are loves that remain seated
in the dim light,
certain,
almost severe in their devotion.

Mine is the latter.

And if ever you doubt your worth —
remember this:

It is not the perfect note
that changes the song.

It is the blue one.

I don't think I say this enough, so I'm writing it instead.

I appreciate the way you exist.

Not loudly.
Not in a way that tries to own the room.
But in the way a blue note bends a melody — subtle, a little aching, and suddenly everything feels more honest because of it.

You move through things quietly, even when they're heavy.
You don't dramatize your strength.
You just carry it.

I respect your pace.
Your "slow."
Your "not yet."
The way you refuse to rush something just because the world expects you to.

That's not distance.
That's self-knowledge.

There's something steady about you, Artemis.
Not cold. Not distant.
Just grounded in who you are.

You don't try to be someone's rescue.
You don't pretend to be perfect.
You're real — and that kind of realness is rare.

You've become like a blue note in my life.
Not loud, not demanding attention.
Just the tone that makes everything else sound truer.

I don't need speed.
I don't need promises.

I just want you to know this is genuine:

You matter to me.
Not because of what you give.
Not because of what you fix.
Just because you're you.

And I'm grateful I get to know you.
